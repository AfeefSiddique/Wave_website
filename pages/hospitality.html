Comprehensive Curriculum

Our 12-week intensive program covers all essential aspects of hospitality management.

1

Introduction to Hospitality Industry

Overview of the hospitality sector including hotels, restaurants, tourism, and events. Understanding industry structure, career paths, and global trends. History and evolution of hospitality. Current market analysis and future outlook.

Week 1-2 | 12 hours

2

Front Office Operations

Hotel reception management, reservation systems, check-in/check-out procedures, guest relations, handling complaints, telephone etiquette, and communication protocols. Practical training with hotel management software.

Week 3-4 | 16 hours

3

Food & Beverage Service

Restaurant service standards, menu knowledge, table service techniques, bar operations, wine service, banquet and catering management. Understanding different cuisines and dietary requirements. Health and safety regulations.

Week 5-6 | 16 hours

4

Housekeeping Management

Room cleaning procedures, laundry operations, maintenance coordination, inventory management, quality control standards. Understanding international housekeeping standards and guest preferences.

Week 7 | 8 hours

5

Customer Service Excellence

Professional communication skills, handling difficult situations, customer psychology, creating memorable experiences, building guest loyalty. Cultural sensitivity and working with international guests.

Week 8 | 10 hours

6

Hospitality English

Industry-specific vocabulary, professional communication, email writing, telephone conversations, handling reservations in English, describing services and facilities. Accent training and pronunciation for clear communication.

Week 9-10 | 16 hours

7

Professional Development

Resume building, interview preparation, professional grooming and appearance, workplace etiquette, career planning, networking skills. Understanding different hospitality roles and career progression paths.

Week 11 | 8 hours

8

Practical Training & Assessment

Hands-on training through role-plays and simulations. Mock hotel scenarios, restaurant service practice, handling guest interactions. Final assessment and certification. Job placement assistance and industry connections.

Week 12 | 12 hours

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need prior experience in hospitality?

No prior experience required. This is a foundational program designed for beginners. Whether you're a fresh graduate or changing careers, our course starts from the basics and builds comprehensive skills.

Q: Will I get hands-on training?

Yes! Our program includes extensive practical training through role-plays, simulations, and real-world scenarios. We recreate hotel reception desks, restaurant service situations, and guest interaction scenarios for hands-on practice.

Q: Can this help me get jobs abroad?

Absolutely. Our training aligns with international hospitality standards. Many graduates have successfully secured positions in hotels and cruise ships in Middle East, Europe, and Southeast Asia. We also provide guidance on work visa processes.

Q: What's the success rate for job placement?

Over 85% of our graduates find employment within 3 months of course completion. Our industry connections and dedicated placement support significantly increase job prospects. Success depends on individual performance and job market conditions.

Q: Is English proficiency training included?

Yes! Two weeks of the program are dedicated to Hospitality English, covering industry vocabulary, professional communication, and accent training. However, basic English understanding is required to enroll in the program.
