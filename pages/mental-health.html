Self-Care Tips for Students

Small daily practices can make a big difference in your mental well-being:

Physical Health

  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep
  • Exercise regularly (even 20 minutes helps)
  • Eat nutritious meals
  • Stay hydrated throughout the day

Mental Wellness

  • Practice mindfulness or meditation
  • Take regular study breaks
  • Limit social media use
  • Journal your thoughts and feelings

Social Connection

  • Stay connected with family and friends
  • Join clubs or study groups
  • Don't isolate yourself
  • Ask for help when needed

Balance & Boundaries

  • Set realistic goals and expectations
  • Learn to say no when overwhelmed
  • Schedule time for hobbies and fun
  • Celebrate small achievements

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is counseling really confidential?

Yes, absolutely. Everything you share in counseling sessions remains completely confidential. We only break confidentiality in cases where there is risk of harm to yourself or others, as required by professional ethics.

Q: Do I have to have a "serious problem" to seek help?

Not at all. You don't need to be in crisis to benefit from counseling. Many students seek support for everyday stress, life transitions, or simply to talk through their feelings. Early support can prevent small concerns from becoming bigger issues.

Q: What if I'm too busy to attend sessions?

We understand students have busy schedules. We offer flexible appointment times, including evenings and weekends when possible. Even one session can provide helpful tools and strategies. Your mental health is worth prioritizing.

Q: Will this affect my academic record or applications?

No. Seeking mental health support is completely separate from your academic record. Universities and employers do not have access to counseling information. Taking care of your mental health shows maturity and self-awareness.

Q: What if I need help outside of office hours?

For urgent situations outside our hours, please contact local emergency services or crisis hotlines. We can provide you with a list of 24/7 mental health resources. Your safety and well-being are the top priority.
